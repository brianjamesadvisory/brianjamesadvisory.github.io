Admit It

April 2, 2018 in Food For Thought

My brothers, today I will be talking about one thing, one action, that is

“Admit it.”

Yes, that is the way which helped me achieve a lot of successes, not just with ladies. In the years living this life, I have never met a strong person with an easy past. And, a man would never go from zero to hero if he does not overcome one challenge, just to admit he is weak at it, and needs help.

Interesting yeah?

Back in the day I have to admit that I was terrible in terms of communication. Girls usually had such weird and funny reactions like, “Ha? What? Pardon me?” when engaging in a conversation with me. They had to keep asking again and again because they did not understand the message I was trying to convey.

Yes, I admit that I was dead shy and quiet with ladies. I recall there was a time I had a crush on a girl. I kept following her with a desiring and loving heart. However I couldn’t open up since I was too nervous. Sign

I really have to say, for a man, career is a very important part of life. Nevertheless, lacking of ability to win ladies, the rest of his life is just merely a boredom. You know, that sucks.

I kept on admitting, then admitting, all over again, from mistake to mistake. One by one, day by day.

And you know what, I never blame anyone else. I never hid or kept the truth secret. If I am bad at something, I have to learn it up. Simple!

So I want you to do the same thing. Let’s start admitting it yourself. There is an idiom “Elephant in the room” which means there is something obvious enough, a risk, a potential threat, but nobody pays attention, no one wants to discuss. Everybody ignores it. Don’t be that person. If you admit it, face it, you will overcome it.

I have a Tip for you.

This one is very simple. Just load all your fears and anxieties to your head, then immediately tell someone else. For example, I was seriously shy and embarrassed with girls, I told them oftentimes: “Dear, you see? You made me so nervous. But I am gathering all my courages just to talk to you.”

Or

“I am a bit worrying. But I honestly want to talk to you, in person.”

My friend, those words are innocent, naive, and raw. But they are filled with honesty.

One day, I received a sincere encouragement from a girl. “What do you need? Please slow it down, dear. In the classroom they said that I am shy too. Hence there is no need to be nervous.”

The path from zero to hero is on-going and endless. But bit by bit, day by day, I grew up and became a relationship advisor. Well, who knows, this could be your starting point of a journey of your name, your destiny.

Your friend,

Brian.
